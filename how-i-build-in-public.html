How I Build in Public – Lessons from SVM Labs

As the founder of SVM Labs, I’ve made a commitment: to build in public. Every project, every experiment, every failure and success is visible to anyone who wants to learn or collaborate. Why? Because transparency accelerates learning and builds credibility.

Why I Build in Public

Starting from zero tech knowledge, I realized that waiting for perfect skills or funding would only delay action. By documenting my journey publicly:

  • I hold myself accountable.
  • I share real insights, not just theory.
  • I create opportunities for feedback and collaboration.

Lessons Learned So Far

1. Ship early, ship often: A live version, even imperfect, is better than an idea in your head.

2. Consistency beats intensity: Daily or weekly commits, blog posts, or micro-updates compound over time.

3. Protect creator rights: Every project, every post, every article I publish is timestamped and clearly credited.

Takeaways for Fellow Builders

  • Start with one small project. Don’t wait for perfection.
  • Use public platforms to share progress and build credibility.
  • Focus on value for others, not just recognition.
  • Document learnings: future you will thank past you.

Next Steps

At SVM Labs, my focus is building a platform where writers and readers can connect while respecting intellectual property. The journey has just begun, but by publishing in public, I can refine the vision, gather feedback, and iterate faster.

If you’re building anything, I encourage you to share your progress too. Build in public, stay accountable, and let your work speak.
